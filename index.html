01

So, is it good?

Three reasons 63% is exactly where an honest NBA model should land.

Las Vegas — with insider injury news, sharp money, and decades of modeling — tops out around 66–68%. This model reaches 63% on data it has never seen. The gap is small, and it closes honestly.

With leakage-free rolling features, single-game prediction tops out near 63%. Pushing the model harder — bigger, deeper, less regularized — did not move it. That's the signal ceiling of the data, not a tuning failure.

If this returned 90%, the right response is suspicion, not celebration — a feature secretly encoding the result. Earlier versions leaked through head-to-head and season win-percentage stats. Cleaning them out brought it back to an honest 63%.

Training accuracy (62.8%) and held-out test accuracy (62.9%) sit within a tenth of a point. The model isn't memorizing the past; it generalizes to seasons it never saw. Heavy dropout and weight decay keep it there.
